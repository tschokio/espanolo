# Curriculum Map

This document translates the learning roadmap into units that can be built, seeded, tested, and shipped. Each unit should contain lessons, vocabulary, exercises, a checkpoint, and review rules.

## Lesson Design Standard

Every lesson should include:

- One clear communicative goal.
- One grammar target.
- One vocabulary target.
- Three to six model sentences.
- Active recall practice, not only recognition.
- A short production task.
- Review summary and weak-pattern tags.

Example:

```text
Goal: Order one item politely.
Grammar: quiero + noun, un/una.
Vocabulary: cafe, agua, pan, manzana, cuenta.
Production: Type "I want a coffee, please" in Spanish.
```

## Checkpoint Standard

Each checkpoint should test:

- Vocabulary recall.
- Grammar choice.
- Sentence building.
- Short translation from English to Spanish.
- One realistic dialogue response.
- Mistake review after completion.

Checkpoints should be mixed and should not reveal the full answer list before the learner answers.

## A1 Units

### A1.0 Absolute Start

Purpose: make the first sessions usable for a learner with almost no Spanish.

Lesson targets:

- yo, tu, el, ella.
- hola, gracias, por favor, perdon.
- soy, estoy, es, esta.
- me llamo, soy de.
- no entiendo, mas despacio.

Checkpoint: introduce yourself and repair a conversation when lost.

### A1.1 Identity and People

Lesson targets:

- ser for identity, origin, role, and basic traits.
- subject pronouns.
- names, family, people, professions.
- adjective agreement for people.

Checkpoint: describe yourself and another person.

### A1.2 Articles, Objects, and Gender

Lesson targets:

- el, la, un, una.
- masculine/feminine nouns.
- classroom, home, and food objects.
- simple noun phrases.

Checkpoint: choose and type correct article + noun phrases.

### A1.3 Location and Estar

Lesson targets:

- estar for location.
- en, cerca, lejos, encima, debajo, delante, detras.
- home, city, classroom, travel places.

Checkpoint: say where people and objects are.

### A1.4 Present Tense Basics

Lesson targets:

- regular -ar verbs.
- regular -er and -ir verbs.
- hablar, estudiar, trabajar, comprar, comer, vivir.
- yo, tu, el/ella, nosotros, ellos forms.

Checkpoint: conjugate and build simple present-tense sentences.

### A1.5 Needs, Wants, and Survival

Lesson targets:

- quiero, necesito, tengo.
- no before the verb.
- donde, que, cuanto, quien.
- food, travel, help, directions.

Checkpoint: restaurant, market, and travel survival quiz.

### A1.6 Description and Agreement

Lesson targets:

- adjective position and agreement.
- colors, numbers, plurals.
- ser for stable descriptions.
- estar for current condition.

Checkpoint: describe objects and people with correct agreement.

### A1.7 Health, Weather, and Daily Situations

Lesson targets:

- me duele, tengo hambre, tengo sed, tengo frio, tengo calor.
- hace sol, llueve, hace frio.
- body, weather, time, basic daily actions.

Checkpoint: explain a simple health/weather/daily-life situation.

## A2 Units

### A2.1 Daily Routine and Time

Lesson targets:

- days, months, time, frequency.
- daily routine verbs.
- reflexive verbs introduction.
- siempre, a veces, nunca, hoy, manana, ayer.

Checkpoint: describe a normal day.

### A2.2 Irregular Present and Useful Verb Frames

Lesson targets:

- ir, hacer, decir, venir, salir, poner, traer.
- poder, querer, tener que, necesitar + infinitive.
- ir a + infinitive.

Checkpoint: explain what you can, want, need, and plan to do.

### A2.3 Likes and Preferences

Lesson targets:

- gustar, encantar, preferir.
- me gusta, te gusta, le gusta, nos gusta.
- hobbies, food, places, activities.

Checkpoint: talk about preferences and ask someone else.

### A2.4 Scenarios and Input

Lesson targets:

- restaurant ordering, recommendations, and bill requests.
- travel directions and conversation repair.
- pharmacy symptoms, medicine, and safety details.
- short reading and listening labs with comprehension checks.

Checkpoint: complete first scenario and input-comprehension checks.

### A2.5 Objects and Pronouns

Lesson targets:

- direct objects: lo, la, los, las.
- indirect objects: me, te, le, nos.
- common placement rules.
- pedir, dar, comprar, ver, tener.

Checkpoint: replace nouns with pronouns in short sentences.

### A2.6 Past Events

Lesson targets:

- preterite regular verbs.
- high-frequency irregular preterite forms.
- ayer, la semana pasada, anoche.
- simple story sequence.

Checkpoint: tell what happened yesterday.

### A2.7 Background and Habitual Past

Lesson targets:

- imperfect: era, estaba, tenía, iba, hablaba.
- descriptions, age, repeated past actions.
- preterite vs imperfect first contrast.

Checkpoint: describe what something was like and what happened.

### A2.8 Expanded Practical Scenarios

Lesson targets:

- hotel, transport, doctor, shopping, and plan changes.
- complaints and problems.
- polite requests.
- basic commands.

Checkpoint: complete longer scenario dialogues.

## B1 Units

### B1.1 Past Tense Control

Lesson targets:

- preterite vs imperfect in stories.
- present perfect.
- time markers and connectors.

Checkpoint: write and understand a short personal story.

### B1.2 Opinions and Explanations

Lesson targets:

- creo que, pienso que, me parece que.
- porque, aunque, sin embargo, por eso.
- comparisons and reasons.

Checkpoint: give an opinion with reasons.

### B1.3 Subjunctive Introduction

Lesson targets:

- quiero que, espero que, es importante que.
- common present subjunctive forms.
- wishes, needs, recommendations.

Checkpoint: choose indicative or subjunctive in controlled sentences.

### B1.4 Reading and Listening

Lesson targets:

- graded articles.
- transcript-based listening.
- comprehension questions.
- vocabulary extraction.

Checkpoint: read or listen and answer in Spanish.

### B1.5 Real-World Communication

Lesson targets:

- explain a problem.
- make arrangements.
- negotiate, apologize, clarify.
- retell information.

Checkpoint: multi-turn dialogue and written response.

## B2 and Advanced Units

Advanced units should be topic-based and skill-based:

- Work and professional Spanish.
- Travel depth and cultural situations.
- News, society, and opinion.
- Health and appointments.
- Relationships and emotions.
- Idioms and native expressions.
- Advanced subjunctive, conditionals, and nuance.
- Long-form reading and writing.

Each advanced unit should produce vocabulary, grammar review, reading, listening, and writing prompts.
